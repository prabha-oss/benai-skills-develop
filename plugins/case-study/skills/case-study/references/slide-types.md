# Slide Types Library

This is NOT a fixed structure. It's a menu of slide types to compose from.
The user and AI together decide which slides to include and in what order.

---

## Opening Slides

### Title Slide
- **Purpose:** First impression. Sets the tone.
- **Elements:** Client logo, case study title, your company logo, tagline
- **Layout:** Clean, minimal. Logo centered or top-left. Title large and centered.
- **Background:** Dark or brand primary color works best.
- **Tip:** Include a subtle visual ‚Äî gradient, abstract shape, or client's product screenshot at low opacity.

### Executive Summary
- **Purpose:** Give the busy reader the TL;DR upfront.
- **Elements:** Challenge overview (2-3 cards), key outcomes (3-5 metrics), pull quote
- **Layout:** Three-column grid for challenges. Metrics as large stat callouts below.
- **When to use:** Always recommended. Sets expectations for what follows.

### Single Challenge Deep-Dive
- **Purpose:** When one challenge deserves its own slide.
- **Elements:** Title, detailed description, impact data, supporting visual
- **Layout:** Split ‚Äî text left, visual/data right
- **When to use:** If there's a particularly dramatic or complex challenge

## Solution Slides

### Objectives
- **Purpose:** Bridge between problem and solution. "Here's what we set out to do."
- **Elements:** 3-5 numbered objectives with descriptions
- **Layout:** Numbered list (01, 02, 03) with bold titles and descriptions
- **Tip:** Use large numbers (01, 02, 03) as visual anchors ‚Äî 36pt+ in accent color

### Actions Taken (Single)
- **Purpose:** Show what was done ‚Äî the meat of the case study.
- **Elements:** 3-5 action items with titles and descriptions
- **Layout:** Icon + title + description rows. Or numbered cards.
- **Tip:** Each action should connect to a specific challenge mentioned earlier

### Actions Taken (Multi-Slide)
- **Purpose:** When actions need more space (evidence, screenshots, detail)
- **Elements:** 1-2 actions per slide with supporting visuals
- **Layout:** Action title + description on left, screenshot/evidence on right
- **When to use:** When you have screenshots, data, or detailed process explanations

### Process / Timeline
- **Purpose:** Show the journey chronologically
- **Elements:** Timeline with milestones, dates, key events
- **Layout:** Horizontal timeline with points, or vertical steps with connectors
- **When to use:** When the sequence matters (e.g., phased rollout, sprint cycles)

## Proof Slides

### Results (Metrics)
- **Purpose:** The payoff. Show the numbers.
- **Elements:** Hero metric (huge), supporting metrics (medium), context
- **Layout:** Hero metric centered at top (60-72pt). Supporting metrics in grid below.
- **Tip:** Always include context: "up from X" or "vs. industry average of Y"
- **Design:** Use accent color for the numbers. Muted color for labels.

### Results (Before/After)
- **Purpose:** Show transformation visually
- **Elements:** Two columns ‚Äî Before vs. After with metrics
- **Layout:** Split screen. Left = before (muted colors), Right = after (vibrant)
- **When to use:** When the contrast is dramatic

### Evidence / Screenshots
- **Purpose:** Prove it happened. Show the work.
- **Elements:** Screenshot(s) with captions
- **Layout:** Full-width image with caption, or 2-3 screenshots in grid
- **When to use:** When visual proof strengthens the story (dashboards, content examples, UI)

### Data Visualization
- **Purpose:** Make complex results digestible
- **Elements:** Chart (bar, line, pie) with clear labels
- **Layout:** Chart centered with title above and takeaway below
- **When to use:** Growth over time, comparisons, distribution data

## Trust Slides

### Client Quote (Full Page)
- **Purpose:** Emotional proof. Let the client speak.
- **Elements:** Large quote text, attribution (name + title), optional headshot
- **Layout:** Quote centered, large italic text (24-28pt). Attribution below.
- **Background:** Subtle ‚Äî light background or brand secondary color
- **Tip:** Use quotation marks as large decorative elements (oversized " in accent color)

### Client Quote (Inline)
- **Purpose:** Add a quote without dedicating a full slide
- **Elements:** Quote as a callout box within a content slide
- **Layout:** Quote box with left accent border, integrated with other content
- **When to use:** When you have multiple quotes but don't want multiple quote slides

### Multiple Testimonials
- **Purpose:** Social proof from several stakeholders
- **Elements:** 2-3 quote cards with names and titles
- **Layout:** Cards in a row, each with quote + attribution
- **When to use:** When you have quotes from different team members or stakeholders

## Closing Slides

### Final Thoughts
- **Purpose:** Wrap up the story. Reinforce the transformation.
- **Elements:** Summary paragraph, key takeaway, optional call to action
- **Layout:** Clean text with emphasis on the main takeaway
- **Tip:** End with forward-looking language, not just a recap

### Next Steps
- **Purpose:** Show ongoing relationship or future plans
- **Elements:** 2-4 next steps or ongoing initiatives
- **Layout:** Simple list or numbered items
- **When to use:** When there's an ongoing engagement worth highlighting

### About Us / Team
- **Purpose:** Introduce who did the work
- **Elements:** Company description, team photos, credentials
- **Layout:** Logo + description + team grid
- **When to use:** If this deck will be shared without other context about your company

### CTA / Contact
- **Purpose:** Drive action. Make it easy to reach out.
- **Elements:** Contact info, website, social links, clear CTA
- **Layout:** Centered contact info with CTA button/banner
- **Background:** Dark or brand primary (bookend with title slide)

## Slide Selection Guidelines

### For a tight sales deck (5-7 slides):
Title ‚Üí Challenges ‚Üí Actions ‚Üí Results ‚Üí Quote ‚Üí CTA

### For a standard case study (8-10 slides):
Title ‚Üí Executive Summary ‚Üí Challenges ‚Üí Objectives ‚Üí Actions ‚Üí Results ‚Üí Quote ‚Üí Conclusion ‚Üí CTA

### For a comprehensive showcase (12-15 slides):
Title ‚Üí Executive Summary ‚Üí About Client ‚Üí Challenges ‚Üí Objectives ‚Üí Actions (multi) ‚Üí Evidence ‚Üí Results ‚Üí Before/After ‚Üí Quote ‚Üí Final Thoughts ‚Üí Next Steps ‚Üí Team ‚Üí CTA

### Always ask the user which structure fits their need.
